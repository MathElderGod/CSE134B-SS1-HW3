As a Cancer Survior:

While attending UCSD in Spring 2021, I started losing weight significantly. I couldn't maintain a healthy diet, as I was losing my ability to eat. By the Summer of 2021, I was diagnosed with Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma . I was going to pass away and leave my pregnant wife, at the time, and my 1-year-old son alone in this world. It hurts when I think about it, and no level of therapy will truly cause those wounds to heal. Miraculously, I kept advocating for myself to doctors to receive chemotherapy treatment.As a result, I went to Scripts Mercy Hospital in San Diego, where I started therapy and got much-needed answers. My cancer took over my entire stomach, and a mass tumor in the upper cardia was closing my esophagus entirely. As I received my first chemotherapy, I couldn't help but think I am so happy to be here to get a fighting chance at continuing to be here for my family!

Fortunately, I beat cancer by Spring 2022. As a result, I am an advocate for Cancer Awareness. One of my desires is for a cure for cancer within my lifetime. As a cancer survivor, I can admit that my empathy has grown significantly more. I try my hardest to fully understand people for who they are and what they stand for! I make sure that every day counts!
