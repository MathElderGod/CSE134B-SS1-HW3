Journeys Through Computer Science: Compression/Decompression and Encryption/Decription.

My journey through the vast landscape of Computer Science has led me to delve into intriguing niches, each with its unique set of challenges and rewards. Two of these exciting fields I've been exploring are compression and decompression via Serialization and Deserialization of Binary Trees, and encryption and decryption with SystemVerilog. They might appear quite different at first, but they're bound by the common threads of data transformation and the exploration of information.

On the one hand, I was drawn to the intricate mechanics of serialization and deserialization of Binary Trees during my time at UCSD. This exploration revolved around encoding the structure of a binary tree into a more compact format for efficient storage or transmission - a process known as serialization. To retrieve the original tree, the compressed data would then be decompressed through deserialization. An example is provided in the above image, in which the serialization and deserialization algorithm was run. As you can see, it is not super good with small files, however, the larger the files the greater the compression. As you can see, I managed to compress an 826KB file and turn it into a 104KB compressed file. This topic was extremely fascinating.

On the other hand, I've also been intrigued by the magic of encryption and decryption through SystemVerilog. The challenge of operating on a hardware description and verification language level has been both a mind-bending puzzle and a delight. I've learned to craft secure systems through encryption and to unlock encrypted data through decryption. The pinnacle of this part of my journey has been successfully decrypting six polynomial encryptions in parallel - a challenge that tested my limits and expanded my capabilities.

While these topics may seem separate, they share a fascinating overlap in the exploration of data manipulation. Both encryption and compression techniques manipulate data in ways that are intended to be reversible. And both have a wide range of applications in areas like data security, storage, and transmission. They show the breadth of what we can achieve with data when we really understand it.

I invite you to visit my Projects page, where I've documented my journey in these fields, showcasing my endeavors and achievements. As my exploration continues, I look forward to sharing my experiences and insights with you.

The Magic of Computer Graphics and My Adventures in Crafting New Realities!

My journey in computer science has always been about exploration and growth. One of the most fascinating territories I've ventured into is the realm of Computer Graphics - an area where the theoretical meets the visual, the mathematical intertwines with the artistic.

At the heart of Computer Graphics, we find linear algebra - a subject I have found truly fascinating. It's an abstract field of mathematics that becomes strikingly tangible when applied to graphics. With every vector I manipulate or matrix I transform, I'm essentially shaping pixels to create my own realities.

During my Computer Graphics course at UCSD, I've had the incredible opportunity to design and develop my own graphical algorithms. I've conjured up Mandelbrot fractals, those stunningly intricate images that capture the essence of mathematical beauty and chaos in a single frame. I've manipulated light via Blinn-Phong's reflection model, bathing my own digitally constructed scenes in various hues and intensities of light.

Beyond the still, I've ventured into the dynamic, creating camera transformations using the Rodrigues rotation algorithm. This process imbued my digital scenes with a sense of movement and perspective, bringing them to life in ways that static images could never accomplish.

I've even journeyed along the smooth curves of splines and Bezier curves, implementing De Casteljau's algorithm to render these shapes. To cap it all, I've delved into the intricate process of shadow mapping, adding depth and realism to my graphical creations.

With each algorithm implemented and each project completed, I've grown immensely as a computer scientist and as a creator. Each one pushed me to new limits, challenging me to understand complex concepts, apply them effectively, and create something beautiful and functional.

This continuous journey into the depths of Computer Graphics has made me appreciate even more how computer science is an incredibly diverse and dynamic field. It's a place where creativity and analytical thinking go hand in hand, creating mesmerizing results. As I continue my journey, I look forward to sharing more of my graphical adventures with you. Please check out my work in projects page! :)

Igniting a Revolution: Future Programmers of America and the Need for STEM Outreach!

As a dedicated computer scientist and a proud veteran, my passion for service extends beyond the front lines to the minds of the future generation. Today, I wish to highlight a cause dear to my heart – the need for more focused and accessible Computer Science and STEM outreach for underprivileged youth in the United States.

STEM education, particularly Computer Science, is no longer a luxury. It's an essential component of a holistic education in the 21st century. The transformative power of technology is indelible, and to shape a future where everyone has an equitable stake in this revolution, we must ensure our youth have the foundational knowledge to not just participate in, but to shape this digital landscape.

Yet, many children, especially those from underprivileged backgrounds, are yet to receive their invite to this revolution. Limited access to resources, lack of awareness, and a shortage of role models in the field have contributed to this digital divide. It's a chasm that continues to widen with time, and we need a concerted, dedicated effort to bridge this gap.

Inspired by the Future Farmers of America (FFA), an organization that has been pivotal in promoting and supporting agricultural education, I propose the creation of a new initiative – the Future Programmers of America (FPA). The FPA would aim to spark a passion for Computer Science and other STEM fields in the hearts of young learners, particularly those from underrepresented backgrounds.

By providing access to resources, mentorship, and creating a community that encourages exploration and learning, the FPA could revolutionize how STEM education is approached in our country. We can transform passive consumers of technology into active contributors and creators, instilling not only the skills necessary for future careers but a sense of agency, empowerment, and confidence in our youth.

As we look forward to the digital future, it's our responsibility to ensure no child is left behind. It is through initiatives like the FPA that we can ignite the flame of curiosity and passion in our youth, paving the way for a generation of innovators, problem solvers, and leaders. This is not merely a dream for a better education system, but a vision for a stronger, more inclusive America.

In serving our youth, we are serving our nation. Let's join hands in this noble mission and continue to serve this beautiful country, one line of code at a time.
